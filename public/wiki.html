Overview

Uranus is the seventh planet and first ice giant. Its most striking feature is an extreme axial tilt of 97.77 degrees, essentially rolling on its side as it orbits the Sun. This causes extreme seasonal variation over an 84-year orbital period. Only one spacecraft has ever visited (Voyager 2, 1986).

Notable features

Extreme axial tilt
97.77 degrees means poles point at the Sun in turn over the orbital period, producing 42-year seasons.
Major moons
Miranda, Ariel, Umbriel, Titania, Oberon. All named after Shakespeare and Pope characters. Miranda has enormous cliffs and varied terrain.
Epsilon ring
Brightest of the 13 rings. Confined by shepherd moons Cordelia and Ophelia.
Cold atmosphere
Least internal heat of the giant planets, ~1/10th the heat output expected.
